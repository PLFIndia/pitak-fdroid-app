# Pitak — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-06-08

Pitak is a free app for managing personal and community libraries. This policy
explains, in plain language, what happens to your data when you use it.

The short version: **Pitak has no server of its own and collects nothing about
you.** Your data lives on your device. The app only contacts outside services
when you ask it to, and those requests go directly from your device to those
services — never through us.

## Who "we" are

Pitak is developed by Parallel Line Foundation. Once you install the app, it
runs entirely on your device. We operate no backend, no account system, no
analytics, and no servers that your copy of the app talks to.

## What we collect

Nothing. We do not collect, receive, store, transmit, or have access to any of
your data. We have no way to see your books, your borrowers, your loans, your
settings, or anything else you put into the app. There are no user accounts with
us, no sign-in with us, and no telemetry.

## Where your data lives

All of your data is stored locally on your device:

- **Books and wishlist** are stored on your device in unencrypted form, because
  these are the lists you may choose to share or publish.
- **Borrowers and loan records** are stored in an encrypted vault on your device,
  protected by your device biometric and your own passphrase. This data never
  leaves your device except inside an encrypted backup that you create and
  control.
- **App settings** (library name, theme, preferences) are stored on your device.

Your backup passphrase and encryption keys are held only on your device. We
cannot recover them for you.

## When the app uses the internet

Pitak uses the internet only for features you actively choose to use, and every
request goes directly from your device to a third-party service you have opted
into. Specifically:

- **Looking up book details** — when you scan or enter an ISBN, the app may query
  public catalogues (Open Library, Google Books) and book-cover image services to
  fill in title, author, and cover. Only the ISBN (or your search terms) is sent.
- **Publishing your library** — if you choose to publish, the app uploads the
  public version of your library to your own GitHub account (and, optionally,
  your own Cloudflare account). These are your accounts, governed by those
  providers' own privacy policies. We are not involved and receive nothing.
- **Contributing translations or sending a report** — if you opt in, the app
  opens a page in your browser to your chosen destination (a public GitHub
  repository). Nothing is sent without your explicit action.

If you never use these features, the app makes no network requests at all.
Borrower and loan data is never sent anywhere by these features.

## What we share

Nothing, because we have nothing. We do not sell, rent, or share your data with
anyone — we never receive it in the first place.

## Permissions the app requests

- **Internet** — for the optional book lookups and publishing described above.
- **Camera** — to scan ISBN barcodes and library QR codes. Camera images are
  processed on your device and are never sent anywhere.
- **Biometric** — to unlock the encrypted vault on your device.

## Children's privacy

Pitak does not collect personal information from anyone, including children.

## Third-party services

When you use a feature that contacts an outside service (Open Library, Google
Books, cover-image providers, GitHub, Cloudflare), that service handles your
request under its own privacy policy. We do not control those services and are
not responsible for them.

## Data retention and deletion

Because your data is on your device, you control it completely. Deleting a record
removes it. Uninstalling the app removes its data from your device. Anything you
published to your own GitHub or Cloudflare account stays there until you remove
it from that account.

## Changes to this policy

We may update this policy. The "last updated" date above will change. Continued
use of the app after an update means you accept the revised policy.

## Contact

For questions about this policy or the app, use the contact or support
information provided on the app's store listing or website.
